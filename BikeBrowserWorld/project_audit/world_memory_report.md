# World Memory Report

Generated: 2026-05-15

## Emotional Target

The pass aims to move the world from `assembled` toward `remembered`: not more clutter, but small evidence that the same people return to these spaces day after day.

## Neighborhood Memory

The street now implies repeated routines:

- kids practice jumps at the curb,
- the same chalk games fade over time,
- bikes lean near the fence often enough to leave dusty marks,
- porch lights and windows glow with a regular evening rhythm,
- desert dust collects at curb and planter edges.

The most important memory cluster is the ramp/curb area: chalk, skid, distance marks, and curb wear now relate to one another instead of feeling like separate decorative props.

## Garage Memory

The garage now implies repeated repair and learning:

- coffee mug ring near the workbench,
- tape residue and scratches where notes/tools have been used,
- grease smear where hands have touched parts,
- worn threshold from frequent in-and-out movement,
- small chain, grip, and valve-cap details around the bike repair zone.

The strongest relationship cluster is the repair bench to repair bike path: notes, tools, tape, grease, light, and part marks now point toward the idea that repairs happen here repeatedly.

## Ambient Life

Ambient movement is intentionally quiet:

- warm lights breathe slightly,
- a small note/sign sways gently,
- dust motes pulse in the garage light,
- plant/sign details move just enough to suggest air.

This should make the world feel active without becoming noisy or distracting.

## Remaining Emotionally Empty Spaces

- Far left/right neighborhood exits still need more region-specific diegetic memory later, such as a faded mine trail marker or river-path wear.
- The house facades would benefit from final painted-in porch/window details so small overlay glows do not have to carry all the warmth.
- Garage dust motes are built from tiny polygons for now; final art could replace them with a soft sprite/particle texture if needed.
- True visual judgement still needs in-editor or live playable screenshots, since headless dummy rendering is not reliable for screenshots in this project.
