# Core Principles

## Emotional Principles

- Calmness over stimulation.
- Warmth over spectacle.
- Familiarity over scale.
- Emotional safety over intensity.
- Neighborhood intimacy over open-world excess.
- Silence and negative space are authored material.

## Gameplay Principles

- Mechanics should teach visually.
- Exploration should feel low-pressure.
- UI should support the world.
- Repair should feel tactile.
- Rewards should reassure, not interrupt.
- First-slice quality matters more than broad-region quantity.

## Visual Principles

- Dusk warmth.
- Soft shadows.
- Readable silhouettes.
- Restraint in detail density.
- Handcrafted over procedural feeling.
- Important interactables must read before text explains them.
- Garage clutter should support the repair focus, not compete with it.

## Swarm Principles

- Cohesion over quantity.
- Restraint over feature growth.
- Merge carefully.
- Preserve emotional continuity.
- Report overlaps before merge.
- Treat removal and simplification as progress.

## Protected Identity

BikeBrowserWorld should feel like a warm Sonoran BMX neighborhood at dusk: safe, tactile, curious, local, and handmade. Any lane change that makes the world louder, denser, or more impressive at the expense of readability should pause for convergence review.
