# Embodied Learning Opportunities

## Purpose

This document maps opportunities for the project to teach through physical intuition rather than explanation. The strongest project identity is a warm dusk BMX neighborhood where players understand mechanics by manipulating them.

## Priority Opportunities

### Brake Compression

- Mechanic: brake lever, cable tension, caliper movement, rim/rotor contact.
- Observable behavior: lever pulls, cable tightens, caliper arms close, wheel slows/stops.
- Player action: press/hold brake input near inspection bike.
- Feedback loop: wheel only spins freely before braking; after brake press, wheel stops or resists.
- Educational value: teaches force transfer and safety verification.
- Tactile clarity: very high.
- Emotional fit: excellent for Mrs. Ramirez; practical, calm, caring.
- Implementation difficulty: medium-low.

### Tire Pressure Check

- Mechanic: tire compression and rebound.
- Observable behavior: soft tire squishes too much; ready tire compresses slightly and rebounds.
- Player action: press tire, release, observe deformation.
- Feedback loop: visual compression plus gentle sound confirms “firm but not hard.”
- Educational value: teaches pressure by feel.
- Tactile clarity: high.
- Emotional fit: warm and safe.
- Implementation difficulty: low.

### Chain Tension and Path

- Mechanic: chain alignment, sprocket teeth, tension.
- Observable behavior: chain climbs sideways when misaligned; tracks smoothly when seated.
- Player action: rotate pedals, inspect chain line, guide chain back.
- Feedback loop: clunk/sideways motion becomes smooth/quiet motion.
- Educational value: teaches drivetrain cause/effect.
- Tactile clarity: high if chain path is readable.
- Emotional fit: core Mr. Chen repair identity.
- Implementation difficulty: medium.

### Pedal-to-Wheel Motion Transfer

- Mechanic: pedal rotation drives chain, chain drives rear wheel.
- Observable behavior: pedal turns, chain moves, wheel spins.
- Player action: rotate pedals before and after repair.
- Feedback loop: broken/slipped state produces clunk; repaired state produces smooth spin.
- Educational value: teaches motion transfer.
- Tactile clarity: high.
- Emotional fit: excellent.
- Implementation difficulty: medium.

### Tire Patch Press-and-Hold

- Mechanic: patch adhesion and sealing.
- Observable behavior: patch flattens, edges settle, tube color/shape stabilizes.
- Player action: hold input over patch area for a short calm duration.
- Feedback loop: releasing early keeps edge lifted; full press seals.
- Educational value: teaches patience and contact pressure.
- Tactile clarity: high.
- Emotional fit: quiet careful repair.
- Implementation difficulty: low-medium.

### Tire Inflation

- Mechanic: air pressure accumulation.
- Observable behavior: pump handle moves, tire rounds out, pressure meter rises.
- Player action: pump repeatedly or rhythmically.
- Feedback loop: stop in green band; over/under pressure gives soft corrective feedback.
- Educational value: teaches pressure range and verification.
- Tactile clarity: high.
- Emotional fit: strong tactile payoff.
- Implementation difficulty: medium.

### Wheel Wobble

- Mechanic: wheel trueness and axle stability.
- Observable behavior: wheel rotates with side-to-side wobble; repair reduces wobble.
- Player action: spin wheel and observe rim path.
- Feedback loop: wobble amplitude drops after adjustment.
- Educational value: teaches observation before repair.
- Tactile clarity: medium-high.
- Emotional fit: good garage inspection moment.
- Implementation difficulty: medium.

### Loose Headset Check

- Mechanic: front fork/headset looseness.
- Observable behavior: handlebar/fork shifts under brake hold.
- Player action: hold brake and rock bike gently.
- Feedback loop: loose headset clicks/shifts; fixed headset stays stable.
- Educational value: teaches hidden mechanical play.
- Tactile clarity: medium.
- Emotional fit: future advanced safety check.
- Implementation difficulty: medium-high.

### Bent Derailleur Observation

- Mechanic: derailleur alignment and chain path.
- Observable behavior: derailleur cage sits crooked; chain approaches sprocket at angle.
- Player action: inspect from side and rotate pedals.
- Feedback loop: alignment improves chain path.
- Educational value: teaches drivetrain geometry.
- Tactile clarity: medium.
- Emotional fit: good Mr. Chen advanced repair.
- Implementation difficulty: medium-high.

### Suspension Compression

- Mechanic: fork compression and rebound.
- Observable behavior: front fork compresses and rebounds.
- Player action: press down on handlebar.
- Feedback loop: smooth rebound vs sticky compression.
- Educational value: teaches damping/springs.
- Tactile clarity: high.
- Emotional fit: later BMX identity expansion.
- Implementation difficulty: medium.

## Bike Anatomy Opportunities

Bike anatomy should be discovered through interaction, not a textbook overlay.

Recommended approach:

- Part labels appear only when the player interacts with or inspects the part.
- Labels should be small, warm, and temporary.
- A part name should connect to a visible behavior: lever pulls cable, cable closes brake, chain moves wheel.
- The player should learn part names because they needed them to understand the mechanic.

Suggested first part names:

- Brake lever
- Brake cable
- Caliper
- Rim or rotor
- Tire
- Inner tube
- Patch
- Pump
- Pedal
- Chain
- Sprocket
- Derailleur

## Strongest First Pass

The best first embodied learning pass is:

1. Brake compression loop.
2. Chain motion transfer loop.
3. Tire inflation loop.
4. Patch press-and-hold loop.

These are highly visible, emotionally aligned, and already present in the vertical slice as prompts. They can replace fake progression without expanding scope.
