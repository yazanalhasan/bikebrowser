# Emotional Heatmap

Generated: 2026-05-16

## Purpose

This map protects emotional rhythm. Not every area should be warm, focused, dense, memorable, and talkative at the same time.

## Zone Types

### Calm Zones

- Neighborhood spawn and street center.
- Quiet road band.
- Garage floor rest band.

These should stay visually and sonically sparse. Calm zones are not unfinished; they are how the game breathes.

### Warm Zones

- Mrs. Ramirez porch/safety area.
- Mr. Chen street side.
- Garage threshold.
- Garage workbench.

Warm zones should be welcoming, but not all of them need glow, dialogue, ambience, and props at once.

### Focus Zones

- Garage repair bike.
- Safety check bike.
- Active dialogue box during direct conversation.

Focus zones should win the first read. Supporting systems should back away when these are active.

### Breathing Zones

- Quiet road band.
- Garage open floor.
- Transition pauses between neighborhood and garage.

Breathing zones should resist new decorative content.

### Overstimulated Zones

- Garage repair/workbench area.
- Repair completion moment.
- Neighborhood garage threshold if foundation and storytelling layers both remain strong.

These need reduction review before more polish.

### Emotionally Weak Zones

- Side regions outside the first slice.
- Tire repair relative to chain repair.

These need careful future harmonization, not immediate feature expansion.

### Visually Dense Zones

- Garage workbench and repair station.
- Neighborhood landmark pockets near garage/ramp/porch.

Dense zones must have one clear focal object and lower-contrast supporting detail.

### Emotionally Memorable Zones

- Garage chain repair.
- Dusk neighborhood street.
- Mrs. Ramirez safety habit.
- Mr. Chen quiet mentorship.

These are the identity anchors. Future changes should protect them, not decorate around them.
