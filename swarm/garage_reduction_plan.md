# Garage Reduction Plan

Generated: 2026-05-16

## Goal

Make the garage feel like a safe creative den with a clear repair focus. The player should read the repair bike/workbench relationship first, then discover supporting details quietly.

## Reduction Classification

| Element | Classification | Reason | Sprint Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Repair bike silhouette and chain states | Keep | This is the tactile heart of the garage and strongest gameplay read | Preserve clarity, scale, and state readability. |
| Workbench as craft support | Keep | Supports handmade repair identity | Keep readable but secondary to active repair bike. |
| Workbench small tool details | Soften | Can become dense and compete with repair bike | Lower contrast, reduce emphasis, or visually group into fewer readable clusters. |
| Storytelling props near repair area | Merge | Props can duplicate what repair bike/workbench already say | Merge redundant tool/part clusters; keep only details that support chain repair or safety habit. |
| Background garage clutter | Soften | Makes room feel lived-in but can erode focal hierarchy | Keep lower contrast than interactables; remove any prop that pulls first read. |
| Repair glints | Move later | Glints are payoff cues and can stack with audio/UI/dialogue | Use only after payoff cue policy chooses glint as primary or approved secondary. |
| Ambient motion | Soften | Motion can make the room feel alive but can also add constant activity | Keep subtle, slow, and away from the repair bike unless it teaches physical state. |
| Reward effects | Soften | Reward should reassure, not dominate the repair object | Keep compact and secondary; avoid big motion or long hold. |
| Dialogue acknowledgment after repair | Move later | Human praise can duplicate UI reward and audio payoff | Use only if reward UI/audio are quiet, and keep it brief. |
| Audio stinger | Soften | Supports tactile success but can become celebratory | Low volume, short duration, no bright fanfare. |
| Lighting on repair bike | Keep | Establishes first read and emotional warmth | Keep strongest warm focus near current repair object. |
| Lighting on workbench | Soften | Supports craft memory but can compete with repair bike | Keep warm but lower intensity than repair-object focus. |
| Duplicate warm pools | Merge | Several pools can make the whole room equally special | Merge into one primary repair pool plus one supporting bench pool. |
| Garage floor rest band | Keep | Provides emotional breathing room | Protect from new props, prompts, and story marks. |
| Tire repair station polish | Move later | Current unevenness can expose prototype energy beside chain repair | Either simplify its presentation or defer parity polish until chain repair is integrated. |
| NPC presence in garage | Soften | Human warmth is valuable but can steal repair focus | Avoid ambient lines during active repair and completion payoff. |
| Prompt visibility near repair | Soften | Prompt is needed but should not be the first read | Show only when actionable and keep compact. |

## First-Read Rule

On entering the garage, the player should read:

1. Warm repair area.
2. Repair bike or active work object.
3. Workbench as supporting context.
4. Other props and NPC presence.
5. UI.

If UI, props, glints, or dialogue are read before the repair area, the garage is overworked.

## Reduction Order

1. Remove or lower duplicate warm pools.
2. Group or soften workbench details.
3. Protect garage floor rest band.
4. Pick one payoff cue for chain repair.
5. Lower reward UI and audio if both are present.
6. Delay NPC acknowledgment until after the repair object has been read.
7. Reassess tire repair for simplification or deferral.

## Manual Inspection Checklist

- Does the repair bike win the first read?
- Does the workbench feel supportive rather than equally important?
- Is there at least one quiet floor/rest area?
- Do prompts appear only when useful?
- Is there only one primary completion cue?
- Does audio leave a breath after completion?
- Does the garage feel safe and handmade rather than busy?

## Merge Gate

Garage changes are not ready unless the lane report names:

- What stayed primary.
- What became softer.
- What was merged or removed.
- What was intentionally moved later.
