# Next Convergence Phase

Updated: 2026-05-16 - Phase 3 Reduction + Harmonization

## 1. Remaining Major Polish Domains

- First-slice reward reliability, with presentation held quiet until validation passes.
- Garage density reduction and focal hierarchy protection.
- Neighborhood quiet-space preservation.
- Combined UI/audio/NPC pacing reduction.
- Tactile repair refinement without added spectacle.
- Tire repair simplification or parity planning relative to chain repair.
- Side-region emotional parity later, after first-slice rhythm is stable.

## 2. Integration Priorities

1. Stabilize validation and reward-intent checks.
2. Fill lane reports for lanes 1-8.
3. Establish a clear baseline for `BikeBrowserWorld/` so protected-system changes are reviewable.
4. Run one combined neighborhood-to-garage playthrough focused on emotional load.
5. Apply reduction candidates before accepting more decorative polish.
6. Preserve quiet zones as authored material.

## 3. Merge Sequencing

- Start with documentation and validation infrastructure.
- Merge the most identity-critical, low-overlap work first: bike readability.
- Merge foundation composition before storytelling, because it defines where visual focus lives.
- Review UI and audio together because both shape pacing and perceived intensity.
- Hold NPC ambient lines until UI/audio cadence is stable.
- Hold extra garage story props until repair focus is confirmed.
- Merge storytelling after reduction, not before.

## 4. High-Risk Overlap Zones

- Garage repair bike/workbench area.
- Neighborhood garage threshold.
- Mrs. Ramirez safety-check area.
- Mr. Chen dialogue and chain-repair start.
- Reward completion moments.
- Ambient proximity zones near NPCs.

## 5. Areas Needing Reduction/Simplification

- Garage props that do not directly support repair, learning, or warmth.
- Duplicate glow pools and memory anchors around the same focal point.
- Prompt styling and timing duplicated across interaction scripts.
- Ambient NPC lines if they trigger too often.
- Completion effects if visual, UI, audio, and dialogue all fire together.
- Micro-delays that make simple interactions feel ceremonially paced.
- Side-region expansion pressure before first-slice cohesion is secure.

## 6. Remaining Prototype Energy

- Some side regions still read simpler than the neighborhood/garage slice.
- Tire repair is less polished than chain repair.
- Some prompts remain raw Label-style presentation.
- Reward-intent validation failures keep completion flow feeling technically unresolved.
- Headless validation cannot yet prove emotional quality.

## 7. Emotional Weak Spots

- Over-explaining through prompts or NPC lines.
- Garage becoming visually busy instead of intimate.
- Neighborhood losing calm travel rhythm.
- Rewards feeling like UI events rather than quiet achievement.
- Side regions feeling like destinations from a different production stage.

## 8. Most Important Next Convergence Pass

Run a full first-slice emotional pacing pass from neighborhood spawn to Mrs. Ramirez, Mr. Chen, garage chain repair, reward, and return. The pass should identify what to remove, lower, delay, or silence before adding anything new.

Success means the player remembers the place, the bike, and the neighbors more than the interface.

## 9. Deprioritized Until Further Notice

- New gameplay systems.
- New regions.
- Large content growth.
- New reward ceremony.
- More ambient NPC lines.
- Additional garage decoration.
- Extra glow/light layers.

## 10. Phase 3 Definition Of Done

- Reduction candidates are reviewed against a combined playthrough.
- Merge queue is updated from production order to emotional-risk order.
- At least one garage or neighborhood element is lowered, merged, or intentionally preserved as negative space before new polish is accepted.
- Lanes 1-8 submit report updates with explicit "what became quieter" notes.
